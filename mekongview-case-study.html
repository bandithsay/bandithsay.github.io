01 — The Problem

Urban growth that priced out quiet living

Phnom Penh’s rapid urban development increased demand for housing in the city center — but it also drove rental prices higher and reduced access to quiet living environments.

Residents and expatriates increasingly wanted two things that were hard to find together: affordable housing alternatives and a quieter, nature-oriented living environment — both within reasonable proximity to the city center.

Most condominiums, however, sat in dense urban areas with limited space and higher rental costs.

“The opportunity wasn’t another city condo. It was resort-style living near the city — affordable enough for residents and attractive enough for investors.”

02 — Product Strategy

Three strategic advantages

As General Manager and Product Lead, I positioned MekongView around three strategic advantages that together differentiated it from typical urban condominiums.

Advantage One

Nature near the city

Sited on the Chroy Changvar peninsula with Mekong and Tonle Sap river views — a retreat-like setting just 10–15 minutes from downtown.

Advantage Two

Residential + condotel

A condotel model serving both long-term residents and short-term visitors — letting owners and investors generate rental income.

Advantage Three

Variety of unit types

Studios through one-, two-, and three-bedroom units and duplex penthouses — matching different customer segments.

Together, these positioned MekongView as a residential product — not just a building — with a clear and defensible market identity.

03 — Product Experience

A multi-property residential ecosystem

The project was designed as a multi-property residential ecosystem rather than a single building — with shared facilities that created a resort-style living experience across the development.

Infinity rooftop swimming pools
River-view sky lounges
Restaurants and recreational spaces
Shared facilities accessible to residents

These amenities were a deliberate differentiation strategy: by designing the experience at the ecosystem level rather than per-building, MekongView could offer something individual urban condos structurally could not.

04 — Target Users

Four distinct customer segments

The product was designed to serve multiple user segments — each with different needs that the mixed-use model and unit variety could address.

Segment 01

Investors

Local property investors seeking rental income and long-term property value.

Segment 02

Urban professionals

Young Cambodian professionals and couples looking for affordable condominiums near the city center.

Segment 03

Expatriates

Foreign residents seeking larger, quieter living spaces outside the dense city center.

Segment 04

Tourists

Short-term visitors and group travelers attracted by the condotel concept and large apartment units.

05 — Go-to-Market

Putting the peninsula on the map

Marketing and sales were designed to raise awareness of the Chroy Changvar peninsula area and highlight MekongView’s lifestyle advantages.

Digital marketing through websites, Facebook, and Google ads
Partnerships with real estate brokers and travel platforms
Events and promotional campaigns to attract expatriates and investors
Positioning MekongView as a weekend getaway destination for Phnom Penh residents

The strategy treated lifestyle — not just square footage — as the thing being sold.

07 — Key Learnings

Treating real estate as a product

Developing real estate as a product requires balancing location strategy, pricing, lifestyle positioning, and operational management together — not as separate workstreams.

1

Lifestyle positioning differentiates. Lifestyle — nature, river views, a retreat feel — can set a residential project apart from typical city condos far more effectively than price or specs alone.

2

Ecosystems beat isolated buildings. A multi-property ecosystem with shared amenities creates stronger brand value than any single building could on its own.

3

Experience marketing builds markets. Experiential marketing and events increased property awareness and investor interest — helping put an entire peninsula on buyers’ maps.

4

One product, many segments. Designing unit variety and a mixed residential-condotel model let a single development serve investors, professionals, expatriates, and tourists at once.
