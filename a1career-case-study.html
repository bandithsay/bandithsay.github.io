01 — The Problem

Recruitment built on passive waiting

Recruitment in Cambodia has traditionally relied on job advertisement websites — companies post vacancies and wait for candidates to respond. That passive model creates friction on both sides of the market.

For employers

Writing lengthy job descriptions, waiting for responses, then spending significant time filtering many unsuitable applicants. Small businesses without HR departments struggle most.

For job seekers

Candidates miss opportunities simply because they aren’t actively searching at the moment a relevant job is posted — good matches never meet.

The result: hiring that is slow and inefficient for everyone. The market needed a structurally different approach — not a better job board, but a better matching system.

02 — Product Vision

From passive advertising to active matching

A1Career was designed to restructure recruitment from passive job advertising into active candidate matching. Instead of waiting for applicants, employers define hiring requirements and the platform identifies suitable candidates from a database of registered professionals.

“The shift was conceptual, not cosmetic: stop asking job seekers to find jobs, and start asking the system to find the match — continuously, even when candidates aren’t looking.”

As Co-Founder and Product Strategist, I led the product concept, business model design, feature definition, and go-to-market strategy. The platform aimed to reduce hiring time, improve candidate quality, simplify applications, and create continuous matching even when candidates were not actively searching.

03 — Product Strategy

A two-sided recruitment marketplace

The strategy focused on building a genuine two-sided marketplace — each side needed its own carefully designed experience for the matching engine to have value.

Side One

Job Seeker Experience

A guided system that simplified profile creation — guided CV creation tool, professional profiles with skills and achievements, job matching notifications, and video/online interview capability. Candidates stay discoverable even when not actively searching.

Side Two

Employer Experience

Tools to reduce recruitment complexity — job description templates, a candidate matching system, online screening tools, multi-user company accounts for department heads, and a dashboard to manage multiple postings and applicants.

Designing both sides in parallel was essential: a matching engine is only as valuable as the quality and depth of profiles on each side of it.

04 — Product Features

Core features that streamline hiring

Candidate Matching Engine

Employers specify hiring requirements and the system generates a list of suitable candidates from the job seeker database — the core of the active-matching model.

Digital CV Builder

Candidates create structured CV profiles using guided prompts — allowing users without strong English writing skills to still build professional, discoverable profiles.

Company Dashboard

Employers manage multiple job vacancies and candidate pipelines from a single interface — bringing structure to what was previously a scattered process.

Virtual Interview Room

Employers conduct initial interviews through in-app communication tools before arranging in-person meetings — compressing the early hiring funnel.

05 — Business Model

Diversified, two-sided monetization

The platform was designed to generate revenue from multiple channels — capturing value from both recruitment services and advertising placements rather than depending on a single stream.

Job advertisement subscriptions

Recurring subscription fees for employers posting vacancies on the platform.

Resume database access

Paid search access to the registered candidate database for active recruiters.

Premium company profiles

Promoted, enhanced company profiles for stronger employer branding.

Display advertising

Advertising placements across the platform’s high-traffic surfaces.

This diversified structure gave the platform multiple paths to revenue while it built the candidate database that made the core product valuable.

06 — Go-to-Market

Build the database, then attract employers

Because a matching platform is only valuable once it has supply, the growth strategy focused first on building a large database of professional job seekers — which would in turn increase the platform’s value to employers.

Campaigns encouraging job seekers to create CV profiles
Social media marketing through Facebook and digital ads
Educational content explaining the new recruitment model to employers
Partnerships with universities and professional communities

The sequencing was deliberate: a deep, high-quality candidate database first — then employer acquisition, with the matching engine as the proof of value.

07 — Impact

A new recruitment model for Cambodia

A1Career introduced a new recruitment model in Cambodia by focusing on candidate matching rather than passive job advertisements.

The platform demonstrated how recruitment technology could reduce hiring time, improve candidate discovery, and provide a more structured hiring experience for companies of all sizes — particularly the small businesses that had been least served by traditional job boards.

08 — Key Learnings

What building a marketplace taught me

Building a digital marketplace product requires balancing both sides of the platform ecosystem at once — neither side has value without the other.

1

Supply comes before demand. Recruitment platforms must build a large candidate database before attracting employers — the matching engine is worthless without depth on the supply side.

2

Onboarding friction is product strategy. Simplifying CV creation significantly improved user onboarding — the guided builder turned a barrier (writing a CV in English) into a few simple steps.

3

Direct communication accelerates the funnel. Enabling employers and candidates to communicate directly in-app increased hiring efficiency and kept activity on the platform.

4

Brand awareness drives adoption in emerging markets. When you’re introducing a genuinely new model, the market first has to understand it — strong brand awareness and education are essential for platform adoption.
